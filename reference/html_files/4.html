Why Segregated Funds Outclass Mutual Funds for Children
By routing child benefit payments into a Segregated Fund, you establish a locked contract guaranteeing that at least 75% of your total inputs are contractually insulated from stock market collapse. It also names the child as beneficiary, immediately paying out bypass-probate upon estate transfer.

Named Beneficiary Transfer

If something happens to you, the policy skips probate completely, bypassing expensive legal fees (1.5% - 5% in Canada) to deposit instantly into the child's pocket.

Market Resets

Most Segregated Funds allow you to "reset" your guaranteed floor annually. If your fund doubles, lock in that new peak value as your new permanent minimum.

Creditor Immunity

Because these are insurance contracts, they carry unique creditor insulation, securing your child's educational funds even against personal financial liabilities.

*Disclaimer: Calculations assume monthly compounded investment growth. Returns inside Segregated Funds reflect net returns after Management Expense Ratio (MER) adjustments, which carry a tiny insurance cost premium (typically 0.2% - 0.5% higher than mutual funds) in exchange for the contract safety guarantees. Maturity guarantees typically vest on a 10-year lock-in cycle.
